Term
Definition
Air Transport System
The system of systems within the broad aviation sector including: civil aviation authorities (CAA), air traffic management (ATM), flight operations (FO), aircraft maintenance (MX), original equipment manufacturers (OEM), and airport services (e.g. ground services, baggage handlers, fuel suppliers, catering etc.)
Emergence
How a system’s properties and behaviour arise from the relationships and interactions across parts, and not from the individual parts in isolation or properties of components. 
Resilience
A system is resilient if it can adjust its functioning prior to, during, or following events (changes, disturbances, and opportunities), and thereby sustain required operations under both expected and unexpected conditions. (Hollnagel, 2014)
Safety & Resilience
Resilience Engineering sees safety as “the ability to succeed under varying conditions” (Hollnagel, 2011)
Safety indicator
Proxy measures for items identified as important in the underlying model(s) of safety. As such they are uncertain and often only distantly connected to the idealized measures that rarely available in practice. (Wreathall, 2009)
Indicators & potential for resilience
Resilience Engineering is concerned with monitoring and managing performance at the boundaries of competence/operation under changing demands (adapted from Mendoca).
Resilience patterns
  1. Resilience systems are able to recognise that the adaptive capacity is falling (signs that the system is exhausting its capacity to adapt)
  2. Resilience systems are able to recognise the threat of exhausting buffers and reserves
  3. Resilience systems are able to recognise when to shift priorities across goal trade-offs
  4. Resilience systems are able to make perspective shifts and contrast different perspectives that go beyond their nominal position
  5. Resilience systems are able to navigate interdependencies across roles, activities, levels
  6. Resilience systems are able to recognise the need to learn and adapt
(Dealing with the potential, resilience and the ability to anticipate, Woods, 2011 In Resilience Engineering in Practice)
