<b>Scope of project</b><br /></p><p>SCALES is a SESAR WP-E project which started in Autumn 2013 and it will end in Spring 2016. The central goal of SCALES is to link Resilience Engineering and Enterprise Architecture principles into a framework (the "SCALES Framework") that enables a context driven analysis of resilience. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p><b>The SCALES Framework</b><br /></p><p>Enterprise architecture offers the opportunity to model system of systems and to consider the system from different viewpoints (functional, communication, information and process view). For each viewpoint SCALES will identify appropriate resilience related indicators. These will be measured and monitored during system in operation, providing information about system ability to adapt to perturbations and maintain its functionality in specific cases. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The current scope of the framework is go-around situations and with an emphasis on ATM.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><b>How to navigate in the SCALES Framework</b><br /></p><p>The SCALES Framework is composed of four essential viewpoints:<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><ul><li>The Functional Viewpoint: Shows relevant roles and actors and their functions / responsibilities related to the target system. </li><br /></p><p></ul><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><ul><li>The Process Viewpoint: Shows processes and interactions among roles and actors.</li><br /></p><p></ul><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><ul><li>The Information Viewpoint: Details the information elements being exchanged in the Process Viewpoint.</li><br /></p><p></ul><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><ul><li>The Resilience Viewpoint: Consists of resilience indicators and resilience patterns extracted from the analysis of the other viewpoints.</li><br /></p><p></ul><br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The viewpoints can also be accesses through the menu on the left-hand side. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p><b>Guidelines on the use of viewpoints and architectural notation</b><br /></p><p>Guidelines on how to understand the architectural notation used in the SCALES Framework can be found here:<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><b><br /></p><p></b><b><br /></p><p></b><b>Relation to European ATM Architecture (EATMA)</b><br /></p><p>The SCALES Framework aims to harmonize with the European ATM Architecture (EATMA). EATMA is a navigable web architecture and is decomposed into viewpoints similar to the SCALES Framework. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>The purpose of EATMA is to:<br /></p><p><ul><li>Optimise research and development during the SESAR Development Phase, and to</li><br /></p><p><li>Optimise the deployment of the developed concepts and tools to ensure that they improve ATM performance against the targets set by the European Commission</li><br /></p><p></ul><br /></p><p>In order to cope with this complexity with having an architecture acting as both a planning and coordination instrument and global repository of knowledge from SESAR projects the EATMA is structured into layers maintaining the different responsibility areas. Most relevant for SCALES is the operational architecture and in SCALES we will try to align our architectural components according to EATMA in order to provide useful input with respect to resilience that is consistent with other work within SESAR. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>A representation of the operational architecture in EATMA is included here:<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><b><br /></p><p></b><b><br /></p><p></b><b>Link to Diagram's Report</b><br /></p><p>All diagrams can be downloaded as PDF from the following link:<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><b><br /></p><p></b><b><br /></p><p></b><b>Terms and abbreviations </b><br /></p><p>Terms and abbreviations used in the SCALES Framework can be found here: <br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p><b><br /></p><p></b><b>License</b><br /></p><p>The SCALES Framework is licensed under a <br /></p>
